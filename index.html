Here's a timeline of Steve Jobs' most important life events:

1955

Steve Jobs was born and adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.

1968

Steve Jobs worked at HP after talking to co-founder Bill Hewlett.

1969

Steve Jobs met Steve Wozniak and shared their insterest of electronics.

1972

Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak built and illegally sold Blue Boxes which enabled free phone calls.

1973

Steve Jobs enrolled at Reed College but dropped out very quickly.

1976

Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne established the Apple computer company.

1980

Apple entered the stock market and went public.

1984

Apple introduced the Macintosh.

1985

Steve Jobs was fired from Apple but then founded another computer company called NeXT.

1986

Steve Jobs acquired the computer animation division of George Lucas' ILM and incorporated it as Pixar.

1997

Apple acquired the NeXT operating system and Steve Jobs returned to his former role as CEO.

2001

Apple introduced the iPod and changed how music is listened today.

2003

Steve Jobs was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer and had surgery.

2007

Apple introduced the iPhone and changed the technology industry.

2009

Steve Jobs took a medical leave of absence and had a liver transplant.

2010

Apple introduced the iPad and changed the tablet market.

2011

Steve Jobs resigned from Apple and soon after passed away.
